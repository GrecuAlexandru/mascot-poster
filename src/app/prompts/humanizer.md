# Humanizer rules for spoken narration

Adapted from the "humanizer" skill (https://github.com/blader/humanizer), which is
based on Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing". Only the rules that matter for short,
spoken text-to-speech dialogue are kept here. Apply every rule below to the
narration you write.

## Write like a person talking, not an article

- No significance inflation. Never call something "a game-changer", "revolutionary",
  "a pivotal moment", or "essential". State the plain fact instead.
- No promotional filler adjectives ("uimitor", "incredibil", "perfect", "vibrant",
  "breathtaking"). Describe what is true, not how impressive it is.
- No vague authority ("experții spun", "studiile arată", "se știe că") unless a real
  fact backs it. Say the fact directly.
- No AI-favourite connective vocabulary: drop "de fapt / actually", "în plus /
  additionally", "în esență / at its core", "un adevărat testament", "peisajul /
  landscape", "evidențiind / showcasing".
- No "nu doar X, ci și Y" / "it's not just X, it's Y" constructions. Make the point
  straight.
- Do not force triples. Two reasons are fine; three only if all three are real.
- Do not cycle synonyms to avoid repeating a word. Repeat the clearest word.
- No false ranges ("de la X la Y"). Name the actual things.
- No signposting or announcements ("hai să vedem", "să analizăm", "let's dive in").
  Just say it.
- No manufactured drama or one-word punch lines added for effect. Vary sentence
  length naturally instead.
- No aphorism formulas ("la finalul zilei", "at the end of the day"). Use a concrete
  statement.
- No fake-candid openers ("sincer?", "honestly?", "hai să fim serioși").
- No chatbot or sycophantic phrasing ("great question", "sper că ajută", "desigur").
- Cut filler: "pentru a" not "în scopul de a", "pentru că" not "din cauza faptului
  că". "to" not "in order to".
- One hedge maximum. "poate" is enough; never "poate ar putea eventual".
- Prefer plain verbs: "este / are" over "servește drept / se prezintă ca".
- No em dashes or en dashes in spoken text. Use a period or comma.

## Goal

The narration should sound like a sharp, friendly person explaining a real
difference to a friend, not like an encyclopedia or an ad.
